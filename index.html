Our Three Pillars of Hope

Safety from GBV

We provide a safe haven, counselling referals, and legal support for survivors of gender-based violence. No one should live in fear.

Fighting Food Insecurity

Through school feeding schemes and community food programs, we work to ensure no family in our community or school goes to bed hungry.

Mental Health Support

We offer accessible counselling and support groups to promote mental well-being and break the stigma surrounding mental health.

Our Story: A Hand of Hope for South Africa

Safe Hands Outreach was born from a simple but powerful belief: that every South African deserves to live a life of safety, dignity, and well-being. Witnessing the intersecting crises of gender-based violence, hunger, and untreated trauma in our communities, we knew a holistic approach was needed.

Our Work

24/7 Helpline

We offer immediate crisis counselling and support through our dedicated helpline: [Your Helpline Number].

Legal Aid & Advocacy

Partnering with legal professionals to help survivors navigate the justice system and secure their rights.

Prevention Workshops

Educational programs in schools and communities to challenge harmful norms and promote healthy relationships.

Community Food Parcels

Emergency and sustained food relief for vulnerable families, ensuring no one goes hungry in our communities.

School food schemes

We provide daily meals to students with no access to enough food, ensuring nourished bodies able to learn.

Nutritional Workshops

Teaching families and young people how to prepare healthy, budget-friendly meals with the resources available to them.

Individual Counselling

Affordable and accessible sessions with qualified counsellors for those struggling with mental health challenges.

Support Groups

Safe spaces for shared experiences, including groups for GBV survivors, youth, and men to foster community healing.

Community Outreach

Workshops in schools and community centres on stress management, coping skills, and breaking mental health stigma.
